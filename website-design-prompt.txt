WEBSITE DESIGN PROMPT — RC Car Reverse Engineering Project
===========================================================

CONTEXT
-------
This is a first-year university engineering project completed at Birmingham City University (BCU).
The project involved reverse engineering a 1/10 scale Electric Radio-Controlled Trophy Truck
(FTX Zorro model) to understand how and why it was designed the way it is.

The website is a personal/portfolio showcase for the project lead: Ryno Meiring (Team Leader, Group 201).


PROJECT SUMMARY
---------------
The team disassembled, analysed, CAD-modelled, reassembled, tested, and improved a 1/10 FTX Zorro
electric RC Trophy Truck. The truck had a 3rd-party brushless motor (not the original) installed
by its previous owner, which caused a critical overheating problem discovered during testing.

The team solved the problem by designing a custom roof air scoop and windshield deflector (3D
prototyped through multiple iterations) to redirect airflow over the motor to cool it down.

KEY PROJECT PHASES (use these as website sections/pages)
---------------------------------------------------------
1. INTRODUCTION / OVERVIEW
   - FTX Zorro 1/10 Electric Trophy Truck chosen for its blend of mechanical and electrical systems
   - Goal: understand design decisions made by the original engineers
   - Team of 4 students from 4 different countries (EDI survey conducted at start)

2. DISASSEMBLY & ANALYSIS
   - Truck disassembled part by part; ~50 key components identified
   - Each part questioned: what does it do? why was it designed this way?
   - Similarities found to real full-size vehicles (driveshaft, suspension, chassis layout)

3. CAD MODELLING (Fusion 360)
   - All ~50 key parts modelled in CAD — approx. 120 hours of total work
   - Colour-coded by system: chassis, suspension, powertrain, steering
   - Quality control process: all parts verified against real measurements
   - Bill of Materials (BOM) created to support assembly/disassembly

4. KEY SYSTEMS EXPLAINED
   a. Chassis — rigid off-road frame designed for high-speed rough terrain
   b. Suspension — double-wishbone style, similar to real trophy trucks; includes sway bars
   c. Powertrain — CVD driveshafts front and rear; central gearbox; 4WD
   d. Electronics:
      - LiPo battery (voltage = speed, mAh = run time)
      - Brushless motor with KV rating (~3000KV × 8.4V = ~25,200 RPM)
      - ESC (Electronic Speed Controller) — programmable
      - BEC (Battery Elimination Circuit) — steps down voltage for servo/receiver
      - Radio receiver & steering servo

5. TESTING & PROBLEM DISCOVERY
   - Motor overheated to 3× ambient temperature during testing
   - Diagnosed using a FLIR E8xt Thermal Camera (thermal imaging)
   - Root cause: 3rd-party motor lacked sufficient torque at startup, causing excess heat

6. IMPROVEMENT DESIGN (Air Scoop & Windshield)
   - Solution: force airflow over the motor using a roof-mounted air scoop
   - Challenge: at speed, airflow bypasses the roof — passes through the windshield opening instead
   - Fix: design a windshield deflector to redirect airflow up into the scoop and down onto the motor
   - Multiple prototypes designed and iterated (Prototype 1 → 2 → 3 → Final)
   - Benefits of cooling: prevents thermal stress, shorter lifespan, performance loss, and failure

7. CONCLUSION / REFLECTION
   - Engineering requires out-of-the-box thinking and teamwork
   - Project management tools (Gantt charts, BOM, tracking spreadsheets) were critical
   - Sharing automotive knowledge across the team improved outcomes
